Meet the Right People To Build With From Day One

Our ecosystem is designed to help accelerate your progress the moment you step into El Salvador. If you're actively building out a project, we help you find the right partners, investors, vendors, and peers to support you on your entrepreneurial journey. If you're motivated to join someone's team, we help you find the right projects where you can contribute your expertise and make a meaningful impact.

Our network runs on a decentralized, AI-powered, Bitcoin-native infrastructure. This sets the environment for you to meet other high-agency builders, participate in high-signal conversations, and plug into a high-trust network that has everything you need to thrive in El Salvador.

How BIES Supports Its Builders

Events

Our workshops, roundtables, and retreats give builders the opportunity to meet and get to know each other beyond the digital realm. Events are hosted in small group settings, where discussions are open, productive, and protected under the Chatham House Rule. Our events bring builders together often enough for relationships to form and trust to build.

Media

Our newsletter and podcast highlight the builders in our community, the projects they're working on, and the journey that brought them to El Salvador. Seeing how others made the trip can give builders the inspiration, confidence, and know-how to navigate the journey themselves. Our newsletter is a way to stay in the loop on projects taking shape across El Salvador.

Digital Community

Our private digital community is our digital HQ, where builders connect, collaborate, coordinate, and interact economically with each other. Members love our chat feed for its high-signal conversations and engagement. When builders are out of the country, our feed helps them stay connected to the BIES ecosystem and its entrepreneurial energy.

About Build in El Salvador

BIES emerged as a solution to the struggles of entrepreneurship in an unfamiliar country, and the pressures of having to navigate the journey alone.

What began as an informal gathering of entrepreneurs and friends became a thriving network of builders, investors, vendors, peers, and government officials coordinating on projects and contributing to the growth and revitalization of El Salvador.
